# HR Scorecard Scoring Fix - Complete Solution

## 🚨 **The Problem**

You reported that the HR scorecard showed inconsistent scoring where:
- **Candidate A**: 88% overall score with **0 matching keywords**
- **Candidate B**: 83% overall score with **12 matching keywords**

This was clearly wrong - keyword matching should significantly impact the final score.

## 🔍 **Root Cause Analysis**

The issue was that **keyword coverage and overall scoring were completely disconnected**:

### 1. **Two Independent Systems**
- **Overall Score**: Generated by LLM based on holistic assessment (experience, skills, culture fit)
- **Keyword Coverage**: Calculated separately by string-matching algorithm and displayed as side information

### 2. **LLM Prompt Issues**
- The prompt explicitly said: `"DO NOT analyze keywords - this will be handled separately"`
- The LLM generated scores based on general qualifications without considering keyword matching
- Keyword analysis was done afterwards and just replaced the empty keyword section

### 3. **No Integration**
- The overall score was never adjusted based on actual keyword matching results
- High scores could be given for general experience even with zero keyword matches

## ✅ **The Complete Solution**

### 1. **Updated LLM Prompt**
- Added `keyword_technical_match` as a mandatory 4th scoring category
- Changed from 3 to 4 categories in all role types
- Instructed LLM to consider keyword alignment in overall scoring
- Added weighted scoring logic: `"Base the overall_match_score on a weighted average of the 4 breakdown scores, with keyword_technical_match having significant weight (25-30%)"`
